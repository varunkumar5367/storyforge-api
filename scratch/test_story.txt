THE LAST CORNER STORE
PART 1A
PROLOGUE: REALITY COLLAPSE

Red warning lights flashed across the ruined shop.

Every shelf was broken.

Every window was cracked.

Outside... reality itself seemed to be falling apart.

The sky flickered like a damaged screen.

Entire buildings appeared... then vanished.

Kang Min-Jun stood alone in the center of the destruction.

A young man with messy raven-black hair, pale skin, tired dark eyes, and a worn dark-green hoodie.

In his hand rested a strange metallic key formed from countless fragments.

The completed key.

The thing he had spent years searching for.

A mechanical voice echoed from beneath the floor.

"FINAL STABILITY FAILURE DETECTED."

"REALITY COLLAPSE IN SIXTY SECONDS."

The ground shook.

A massive hidden door beneath the shop slowly began to open.

White light poured through the gap.

Min-Jun stared into it.

Fear filled his eyes.

"So this is what my parents were trying to stop..."

The countdown reached fifty-nine.

Then everything went dark.

Three Years Earlier...

CHAPTER 1: THIRTY DAYS LEFT

Rain hammered against the roof of the convenience store.

Drip...

Drip...

Drip...

Water leaked through a crack in the ceiling and landed inside a faded blue bucket.

The sound never stopped.

Kang Min-Jun barely noticed it anymore.

At twenty-two years old, his life had become a routine of disappointment.

Wake up.

Open the store.

Wait for customers.

Get none.

Close the store.

Repeat.

The convenience store had once belonged to his parents.

Now it belonged to debt.

Min-Jun stood behind the counter and looked at the nearly empty street outside.

Nobody came.

Nobody ever came.

The bell above the entrance suddenly rang.

His eyes lit up.

Finally.

A customer.

The hope disappeared almost instantly.

A middle-aged man entered carrying a black umbrella.

The debt collector.

Again.

The man's expression remained cold.

"You know why I'm here."

Min-Jun sighed.

"Third time this month."

"It won't be the last."

The collector placed an envelope on the counter.

Min-Jun didn't even need to open it.

He already knew what it contained.

FINAL NOTICE.

Thirty days.

Thirty days before the bank seized the property.

Thirty days before everything disappeared.

The collector looked around the nearly empty shop.

"No customers?"

Min-Jun remained silent.

The man shook his head.

"Your parents worked hard for this place."

Those words hurt more than the notice.

A few moments later, the collector left.

The bell rang again.

Silence returned.

Min-Jun stared at the envelope.

Thirty days.

That number echoed through his mind.

He opened the register.

Counted the money.

Then counted it again.

The result didn't change.

He didn't have enough.

Not even close.

A bitter laugh escaped his lips.

For a moment he looked toward the wall.

A photograph hung there.

His parents smiled back at him.

His father stood behind the counter.

His mother held a box of snacks.

It was an ordinary picture.

Yet it felt like it belonged to another lifetime.

Back when the shop was alive.

Back when customers filled the aisles.

Back when his parents were still here.

Min-Jun looked away.

The memories were becoming harder to bear.

Hours passed.

Only one customer entered.

She bought a bottle of water.

That was all.

By evening the total sales barely covered electricity costs.

The rain continued.

Outside, the sky grew darker.

Inside, the shop felt colder.

Min-Jun checked his phone.

His bank account balance appeared on the screen.

The number was depressing.

A second later another notification arrived.

Payment overdue.

He closed the app immediately.

Enough.

He couldn't keep staring at numbers that only made him feel worse.

After locking the store, Min-Jun changed clothes and headed toward his second job.

Construction work.

The pay was terrible.

The hours were worse.

But debt didn't care.

For four exhausting hours he carried materials through the rain.

By the time he finished, every muscle in his body hurt.

Near midnight he finally returned to the convenience store.

The rain had become a storm.

Thunder rolled across the city.

Lightning flashed overhead.

Min-Jun unlocked the door.

The moment he stepped inside, a strange feeling washed over him.

Something felt wrong.

He couldn't explain why.

The air seemed heavier.

The shop seemed quieter.

As if it was waiting for something.

CHAPTER 2: THE CLOCK

The storm intensified.

Wind rattled the windows.

Thunder shook the walls.

Min-Jun stood behind the counter drinking instant coffee.

The bitter taste barely kept him awake.

Lightning flashed.

The lights flickered.

Once.

Twice.

Then darkness swallowed the store.

A second later the electricity returned.

Min-Jun looked up.

His eyes landed on the old wall clock.

The same clock that had hung there for years.

Tick.

Tick.

Tick.

The second hand continued moving.

Then another lightning strike exploded outside.

The building trembled.

And the clock stopped.

Exactly midnight.

12:00.

The second hand froze.

Min-Jun stared at it.

For several seconds neither he nor the clock moved.

Then he shrugged.

The thing barely worked anyway.

Another crash of thunder echoed outside.

The lights flickered again.

This time the floor vibrated.

Min-Jun frowned.

The vibration didn't feel natural.

It felt mechanical.

As though giant gears were moving somewhere beneath the building.

He listened carefully.

There it was again.

A deep metallic grinding noise.

Slow.

Heavy.

Ancient.

The sound lasted only a few seconds before vanishing.

Min-Jun walked around the shop.

Nothing seemed unusual.

The shelves were normal.

The storage room was normal.

The floor looked normal.

Eventually he gave up.

He was too tired to investigate ghosts and strange sounds.

After locking the door, he grabbed a blanket and lay down behind the counter.

Rain hammered the roof.

Wind howled outside.

The frozen clock stared down at him.

Midnight.

Still midnight.

Min-Jun closed his eyes.

Sleep arrived quickly.

But sometime later...

A sound woke him.

Metal scraping against metal.

Louder than before.

Much louder.

His eyes snapped open.

The sound vanished immediately.

Silence followed.

Min-Jun sat upright.

His heartbeat accelerated.

The store felt different.

Almost alive.

He looked toward the frozen clock.

The hands remained motionless.

12:00.

Outside, the storm had disappeared.

Morning sunlight filtered through the windows.

Min-Jun frowned.

How long had he been asleep?

Something wasn't right.

CHAPTER 3: THE SILENT MORNING

At first it was just a feeling.

A small feeling.

Barely noticeable.

Then Min-Jun realized what it was.

Silence.

Complete silence.

No traffic.

No voices.

No barking dogs.

No engines.

Nothing.

His neighborhood should have been noisy.

Instead it felt abandoned.

Min-Jun stood slowly.

The silence pressed against his ears.

He checked his phone.

No signal.

Not one bar.

That had never happened before.

A knot formed in his stomach.

He walked toward the front window.

Step.

Step.

Step.

His heartbeat grew louder.

Something was wrong.

Very wrong.

Slowly...

Almost unwillingly...

He looked outside.

And forgot how to breathe.

The city was gone.

The road was gone.

Every familiar building had vanished.

In their place stood something impossible.

Gigantic silver towers stretched toward the clouds.

Floating vehicles drifted between skyscrapers.

Bridges made of glowing light connected structures high above the ground.

The entire skyline looked centuries ahead of modern civilization.

Min-Jun blinked.

The city remained.

He rubbed his eyes.

Still there.

His breathing became uneven.

"What..."

The word barely escaped his mouth.

He rushed toward the entrance.

His mind struggled to process what he was seeing.

Dream.

Hallucination.

Mental breakdown.

Any explanation felt more reasonable than reality.

The futuristic city remained.

Beautiful.

Alien.

Impossible.

Min-Jun grabbed the door handle.

And pulled.

The door opened normally.

Fresh air entered the shop.

For a brief moment relief appeared.

Then he stepped forward.

And everything changed.

CHAPTER 4: THE INVISIBLE WALL

The instant Min-Jun crossed the doorway, a powerful force slammed into his chest.

Bang.

Pain exploded through his body.

He stumbled backward.

The door remained open.

Nothing blocked the entrance.

Yet something had hit him.

Min-Jun stared.

Confused.

Then tried again.

Another step.

Another impact.

Bang.

This time he nearly fell.

Fear replaced confusion.

He extended a hand toward the outside world.

An invisible barrier stopped it.

His fingers couldn't pass through.

The air itself had become solid.

"What is this?"

Panic spread through him.

He rushed toward the back exit.

Same result.

Invisible wall.

He tried the storage room window.

Invisible wall.

Every opening.

Every exit.

Every possible route.

Blocked.

The shop had become a prison.

Minutes passed.

Then nearly an hour.

Min-Jun tested everything.

Nothing worked.

Finally he returned to the counter.

Exhausted.

Breathing heavily.

That was when he noticed it.

A small brass key.

It rested beside the register.

The object definitely hadn't been there before.

Min-Jun picked it up.

Cold metal.

Ancient design.

Attached to it was a folded note.

His heartbeat accelerated.

Slowly he unfolded it.

The paper contained only a few lines.

WELCOME, NEW OWNER.

MONTHLY SALES TARGET:
5000 CREDITS

REWARD:
SHOP UPGRADE

FAILURE:
SHOP RESET

Min-Jun stared at the message.

For several seconds his brain stopped functioning.

Credits?

Upgrade?

Reset?

What did any of that mean?

Then something impossible happened.

The words on the paper vanished.

A glowing blue screen appeared above the register.

Floating in midair.

Waiting.

Watching.

Min-Jun froze.

The screen displayed the exact same message.

WELCOME, NEW OWNER.

A second line slowly appeared beneath it.

INITIALIZATION COMPLETE.

The bell above the entrance suddenly rang.

Ding.

Min-Jun spun around.

Someone had entered.

A silhouette stood in the doorway.

Backlit by the impossible city beyond.

And for the first time since waking up...

Min-Jun realized he might finally get some answers.

END OF PART 1A
